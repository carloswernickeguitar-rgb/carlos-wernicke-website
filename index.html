Carlos Wernicke

Carlos Wernicke approaches music as a space of thought and listening. His work centres on the music of Johann Sebastian Bach and the Renaissance repertoire, approached through historical awareness, musical rhetoric, and a constant search for expressive meaning.

Alongside performance, his artistic practice includes transcription, research, and composition, and extends to other repertoires and musical traditions, shaping programmes in which music enters into dialogue with history, the spoken word, and the cultural contexts that surround it.

About

The guitar becomes a medium of resonance and synthesis, capable of allowing these works to speak again with clarity, depth, and inner intensity.

This programme presents selected works by Johann Sebastian Bach interpreted on the guitar, with an approach rooted in historically informed practice, musical rhetoric, and structural clarity. By combining original works and idiomatic transcriptions, it offers a reflective listening experience that highlights the coherence, depth, and expressive restraint characteristic of Bach’s musical language.

Music & Painting of the Spanish Empire
(EN)(ES)
Download dossiers
EN ES

This programme explores the musical and artistic world of the Spanish Renaissance during the reigns of the Catholic Monarchs, Charles V, and Philip II. By placing Renaissance music in dialogue with contemporary painting, it highlights shared expressive ideals—devotion, symbolism, and transcendence— offering a historically grounded perspective on the unity of the arts in sixteenth-century Spain.

This programme examines the cultural world surrounding Emperor Charles V, shaped by the encounter between the Franco-Flemish and Spanish traditions. Through the parallel presentation of music and painting, it reveals a shared humanistic vision in which polyphonic structure, visual symbolism, and introspective expression converge.
